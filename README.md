# HR-Attrition-Risk-Analysis
This project analyzes employee attrition to identify key factors driving workforce turnover and provide actionable insights for HR leadership.


## 🎯 Business Problem

Employee attrition impacts organizational stability, productivity, and recruitment costs.

The company is experiencing a 16.12% attrition rate, but lacks clarity on:

- Which departments are most affected
- Whether salary influences turnover
- If overtime contributes to burnout
- Whether promotion delays increase resignation risk
-How work-life balance affects retention

Without structured analysis, leadership cannot design targeted retention strategies.

## 💡 Solution Approach

A multi-page Power BI dashboard was developed to:

1. Measure overall attrition rate
2. Segment attrition by department, age, and job role
3. Identify key drivers (salary, overtime, promotion delay, work-life balance)
4. Highlight high-risk employee categories

The solution combines KPI monitoring, distribution analysis, and risk segmentation.

## 📈 Key KPIs

- Total Employees: 1,470
- Total Attrition: 237
- Attrition Rate: 16.12%
- Average Age: 37
- Average Salary: 6.5K
- Average Tenure: 7 Years

## 🔍 Key Insights
1. Department Risk

- Sales shows the highest attrition share (37.72%)
- R&D and HR also show notable attrition patterns

2. Age Group Impact

- Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition
- Early-to-mid career professionals are most vulnerable

3. Overtime Impact

- Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition percentages
- Indicates burnout as a major factor

4️. Salary Band Impact

- Low salary band employees show the highest attrition rate (27.2%)
- Compensation plays a strong role in retention

5️. Promotion Delay

- Employees with longer promotion gaps show increased attrition
- Career stagnation is a key driver

6️. Work-Life Balance

- Employees with lower work-life balance ratings exhibit higher exit percentages
- Cultural and workload factors influence retention

## 🧠 Business Recommendations

Based on the findings:

-> Reduce Overtime Dependency

 - Implement workload balancing strategies, especially in Sales.

-> Review Compensation Strategy

 - Focus on retention bonuses or salary adjustments for low-income bands.

->  Improve Career Growth Programs

 - Shorten promotion cycles and improve internal mobility.

-> Strengthen Work-Life Policies

 -Enhance flexible work policies and employee wellbeing initiatives.
